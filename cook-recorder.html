Cook Recorder

School Project: Redesign | UI/UX

Context

Most of us know that government websites can be a pain to use. No seriously. So, I decided to find one of Illinois's best. I decided on my local Recorder's site, which I thought could benefit from a little boost. Here it is below, but feel free to take a look at the original page. Let's see how it goes.

Accessibility Issues

When I landed on the website, a few usability issues caught my eye. Right away, I noticed the unintuitive layout, poor color contrast, conflicting information, and low readability. In addition, using WebAim's Wave, I was able to detect a couple of accessibility issues. Surprisingly, the website was responsive––it replaced its menu with a dropdown icon at smaller screen sizes. As expected, most of the issues involved contrast. Furthermore, there were a few linked images missing alt text.

Low & High-Fidelity Designs

For my design process, I first created low-fidelity wireframes for 3 different scales: mobile, tablet, and desktop. Once I landed on intuitive layouts for those wireframes, I translated my designs to high-fiedlity prototypes. In order to keep consistency across the designs and to make my life easier, I first created a style guide. That style guide is immediately below, and the designs for each scale follow in a side-by-side format.
